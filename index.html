What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a progressive eye condition marked by optic nerve damage due to increased intraocular pressure. It often develops without symptoms, earning it the moniker "the silent thief of sight." Early detection through regular eye exams is crucial. Treatment aims to reduce pressure and preserve vision with medications, laser therapy, or surgery. Prompt intervention is vital to prevent irreversible vision loss.

Do you have Glaucoma?

Symptoms of glaucoma can vary depending on the type and stage of the condition. In its early stages, glaucoma often presents without noticeable symptoms, which is why regular eye exams are crucial for early detection. As the disease progresses, symptoms may include gradual peripheral vision loss, tunnel vision, blurred vision, halos around lights, eye pain, redness, nausea, and headaches, particularly in cases of acute angle-closure glaucoma. However, it's essential to note that not everyone with glaucoma experiences symptoms, making routine eye check-ups essential for timely diagnosis and treatment.

How to cure Glaucoma?

As of now, there isn't any cure to Glaucoma. If you have glaucoma, it's essential to take proactive steps to manage your eye health and prevent further vision loss. Firstly, adhere to your treatment plan prescribed by your healthcare provider, which may include using prescribed eye drops, taking oral medications, or undergoing laser therapy or surgical procedures as recommended. Additionally, attend regular eye check-ups to monitor the progression of the disease and adjust treatment as needed. Be vigilant about controlling other health conditions such as diabetes and hypertension, as these can exacerbate glaucoma.
